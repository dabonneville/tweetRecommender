TweetRecommender

About TweetRecommender

TweetRecommender tries to solve a seemingly simple question: Which tweets are relevant for a given news article?

The use cases are two-fold. First, the authoring journalist of an article could be interested in feedback for his article. Did he mention everything, did he omit anything critical? How is his style, are there any errors? The same question holds true for a critical reader: Is the article she just read a good one, what do others think? What is the relevant background?

The system works on a collection of tweets and articles which it has pre-processed (for performance and modeling reasons.) When a user enters an article into the query interface, there are three different steps to get a ranked list of relevant tweets: First, a set of potentially relevant tweets is gathered. This works by searching for the article’s URL or contents in the collection of tweets. Second, this set is trimmed down again (by, e. g., restricting the search to a date ranging around the article’s time of publication.) Third, the set of tweets is ranked according to one or more scoring functions. These are combined in a classical voting ballot fashion.

This Web application is only one interface to the database and query algorithms. These are readily exposed as a programming library in Python and a command-line interface. Find the full source code on Github.

About Hasso Plattner Institute

The Hasso Plattner Institute for IT Systems Engineering (HPI) in Potsdam is Germany's university excellence center for IT Systems Engineering. It is the only university institute in Germany to offer a Bachelor’s and Master’s program in “IT Systems Engineering,” providing a practical and engineering-oriented alternative to the conventional study of computer science. Current enrollment is at approximately 460 students. Overall there are approximately 10 professors and over 50 lecturers and visiting professors who work at HPI. HPI conducts outstanding university research — as well as research for renowned partners in business and industry. The primary focus is on the foundation and application of large, highly-complex cross-linked systems.
